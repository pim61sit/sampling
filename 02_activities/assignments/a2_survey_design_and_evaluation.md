# Assignment: Questionnaire Design and Sample Evaluation

## Requirements

The goal of this assignment is to practice developing and evaluating sampling materials.

### Part A - Survey Design:

Select one of the scenarios below and design a survey to meet the need(s) outlined in the prompt.

1.	In two to three sentences, describe the purpose of your survey
2.	Describe your target population, sampling frame, sampling units, and overall sampling strategy.
3.	Write a 5-10 question survey to address your chosen scenario below.

##### Scenarios
1.	You work in the Human Resources Department at a large tech company. Over the past few months, the company has been experiencing a high turnover rate across many of its departments, specifically within the entry- and lower-level positions. The company wishes to understand why this turnover is happening, and what changes need to occur to improve employee satisfaction.
2.	You work for a Canadian national political party during a federal election. Throughout the campaign period, your party has seen relatively high approval ratings, but an opposing party is also polling favorably and may still have a chance to win the election. You are one month away from the election and you want to understand what voters want from your party and its leader in order to maintain your lead and eventually win the election.
3.	You are a student researcher in the sociology department at the University of Toronto. You are working on a research project that concerns the relationship between music taste and age. This involves both comparisons between different people of different ages and comparisons of the same individual at different ages during their lifetime. You wish to understand to what extent age influences music taste, specifically as it relates to perceptions of popular music. Your results will be written into an academic paper that you hope to publish.

Senario A: the Human Resource Department
1. The purpose of this survey is to uncover reasons for employees at the entry- and low levels to leave the organization. 
2. The target population is all entry level and low level staff members at the company. The sampling frame work this study uses the company employee registry or similar list at the HR Department in the last 5 years. The sampling unit for this study is staff members at the entry- and low levels. The overall sampling strategy is systematic sampling by selecting every 3rd member in the registry who left the organization.

### Part B - Survey Evaluation:

For the **Canadian General Social Survey on Giving, Volunteering, and Participating, 2018 (cycle 33)**, conducted by Statistics Canada find any and all available documentation for the data gathered and identify and describe the survey features indicated below.

1. Sample type: probability ramdom sampling of a single eligible member of each sampled household, including strategication method.
2. Sample size: 24000 (expected)
3. Target population: all persons 15 years or older living in the ten provinces of Canada, excluding full-time residents of institutions.
4. Sampling frame: A combined frame of landline and cellular numbers from the Census and other administrative sources within Statistics Canada.
5. Survey mode(s):  Long and short interviews depending on being classified as "volunteers". All respondents in the ten provinces were interviewed by telephone or selfcompleted an electronic questionnaire. Samples without telephone were exclueded from the usrvey population. 
6. Timeline: September to December of the survey year, every 5 years.
7. Response rate: Overall response rate is 41.9%
8. Weights: the survey estimation weights were applied to reduce nonresponse bias in the survey. Also, bootstrap weights is included for design-based variance estimation. 
9. Data processing
10. Cleaning, imputation, etc: All imputations were made using donor records selected through a score function. Nine steps were involved in this inputation process. For further information, please see the original survey information.
11. Sources of error: Both sampling and non-sampling errors could be included. For example, estimate based on a sample will vary from sample to sample, and typically they will be different from the results thatwoud have been obtained from the census. For non-sampling errors, these could include common errors such as imperfect coverage and non-resonse.
12. Limitations, known biases, etc the limitations of this survey are related to data accurary presented the above. the limitations include estimations, coverage errors, non-response bias among others. 
13. Link to documentation and any additional sources used: The administrative sources were used, such as income and household composition, to model and adjust nonresponses. The GSS frame was linked to the Census, administrative data and billing files to improve coverage.
